Event 3: FCAJ Meetup

Event Information

  • Event Name: FCAJ Meetup
  • Date: June 13, 2026
  • Location: FCAJ Meetup
  • Role: Attendee

Event Purpose

FCAJ Meetup was organized to share real experiences, career journeys, and practical knowledge from people who have participated in the First Cloud AI Journey community and worked in cloud, AWS, AI, and DevOps-related fields.

The event helped participants understand how students can grow from learning basic cloud knowledge to joining real projects, building professional skills, and developing their careers in the technology industry.

Speakers and Presentation Groups

  • FCAJ community members – learning journey and practical project sharing
  • AWS Community Builder speaker – journey from FCAJ to the AWS ecosystem
  • DevOps Engineer speaker – DevOps role in real working environments

Main Contents

FCAJ Meetup Sharing Session

This sharing session mainly focused on the real journey and experience of community members in the First Cloud AI Journey community.

The presentation used many images to illustrate their learning process, activities, projects, challenges, and achievements. Although the slides contained limited text, the main message was about how students can start from basic knowledge, participate in community activities, and gradually gain experience in cloud computing and AWS.

The session also showed the importance of learning consistently, joining practical programs, working on real projects, and connecting with people in the technology community.

From First Cloud AI Journey to AWS Partner

The session introduced the journey from the First Cloud AI Journey Program, formerly known as First Cloud Journey, to opportunities in the AWS ecosystem. The speaker shared how participating in community programs can help students build technical knowledge, gain experience, and open career opportunities.

The presentation also introduced several career paths in the cloud field, such as:

  • Solutions Architect
  • Head of Solutions Architect
  • DevOps Engineer
  • Platform Engineer
  • Software Engineer

In addition, the speaker introduced the AWS Student Builder Group Program, formerly known as AWS Cloud Clubs, the AWS Community Builder Program, and the value of joining AWS Partners.

One of the key messages from this session was: “Getting the job is just a beginning.” This reminded participants that getting a job is not the final goal, but the beginning of a longer journey of learning, growing, and writing their own career story.

What does a DevOps Engineer really do?

The speaker explained what a DevOps Engineer actually does in real working environments. The session also explained that many people do not intentionally choose DevOps at first, but gradually enter this field through real work and experience.

The presentation also corrected several common misunderstandings about DevOps. Many people think DevOps is only about writing CI/CD pipelines, using Docker and Kubernetes, deploying code, or fixing production issues at midnight. However, the real role of DevOps is broader and includes system design, automation, monitoring, reliability, and collaboration between development and operations teams.

The speaker also shared what beginners should learn first when they want to follow the DevOps path, including:

  • Linux fundamentals
  • Networking fundamentals
  • Python or Golang
  • Git
  • CI/CD
  • Containers
  • How applications are built, tested, deployed, logged, and configured

The speaker emphasized that learners should build small practical projects instead of only reading theory or copying commands.

The session also included several important lessons from real experience:

  • Copying commands does not mean understanding.
  • It is important to understand “why” before “how”.
  • Communication is an important part of the DevOps role.
  • DevOps Engineers do not always need to solve everything alone.
  • Strong fundamentals are more important than only learning tools.
  • A good DevOps Engineer should be curious, think systematically, automate repetitive tasks, and use AI wisely as a supporting tool.

Lessons Learned

After joining this event, I learned several important lessons:

  • I understood more about the real learning journey from students to cloud and AWS-related careers.
  • I learned how community programs such as First Cloud AI Journey can support students in building technical skills and career direction.
  • I gained more understanding of AWS community programs, such as AWS Student Builder Group and AWS Community Builder.
  • I learned about different career paths in cloud computing, including Solutions Architect, DevOps Engineer, Platform Engineer, and Software Engineer.
  • I understood more clearly what a DevOps Engineer actually does in a real working environment.
  • I learned that strong fundamentals, communication skills, and practical projects are very important for career development.

Application to My Work

The knowledge from this event can be applied to my internship and personal learning journey in several ways:

  • Continue learning AWS and cloud computing through practical labs and projects.
  • Improve my understanding of possible career paths in cloud and DevOps.
  • Focus more on fundamentals such as Linux, networking, Git, CI/CD, and containers.
  • Build small projects to better understand how applications work in real environments.
  • Learn how to explain technical ideas clearly and communicate better with team members.
  • Use AI tools to support learning and working, but not depend on them completely.

Event Experience

Participating in FCAJ Meetup was a meaningful experience for me. The event gave me a chance to listen to real stories from people who have learned, worked, and grown through cloud and AWS-related programs.

The sharing sessions helped me understand that a technology career is not built in one day. It requires continuous learning, practical experience, curiosity, and the ability to adapt to new tools and environments.

The DevOps session was especially useful because it helped me understand that DevOps is not only about tools, but also about system thinking, automation, monitoring, reliability, and collaboration.

Overall, FCAJ Meetup helped me gain more motivation and a clearer direction for my AWS learning path, internship progress, and future career development in cloud computing and DevOps.
